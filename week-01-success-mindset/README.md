# Assignment 1. What is something you believe to be true that most people around you would disagree with?

### Rules

* No "safe" answers.
* Must be your real belief (not copied from internet).
* Minimum 50 words.

**Hint:** What do you believe about career, money, learning, discipline, relationships, health, success, life, tech industry, etc. that most people don't agree with?

## Answer


One belief I strongly hold is that job security no longer comes from staying in one role for many years. Instead, it comes from continuously learning new skills and adapting to changes in technology. Many people around me believe that once you have a stable job, there is little need to invest significant time in learning outside work. I disagree. Technology evolves rapidly, especially in infrastructure, cloud computing, and automation. As a Network Engineer, I have realized that networking knowledge alone is no longer enough to remain competitive. Learning DevOps, cloud platforms, Infrastructure as Code, and automation is not just an advantage. It is becoming a necessity. I believe the professionals who consistently improve themselves will always have more opportunities than those who become comfortable with what they already know

**Evidence from my life:** (2–4 lines: what you tried + what happened)

## Truth #1

### Truth

Consistency produces better long-term results than short periods of intense effort

### Evidence from my life

Whenever I dedicated a fixed amount of time each week to learning networking technologies and Linux, I made steady progress and retained more knowledge. In contrast, trying to learn everything over a weekend often left me overwhelmed and forgetting important concepts. Regular study has consistently delivered better outcomes.

---

## Truth #2

### Truth

Practical experience builds confidence faster than theoretical knowledge alone.

### Evidence from my life

Watching tutorials helped me understand concepts, but building home labs, configuring routers, working with Linux servers, and experimenting with cloud environments taught me far more. Every practical exercise exposed mistakes, improved my troubleshooting skills, and made me more confident in applying what I learned.

---

## Truth #3

### Truth

Strong fundamentals make learning advanced technologies much easier.

### Evidence from my life

My background in networking helped me understand cloud networking, DNS, routing, load balancing, VPNs, and security much faster than someone starting from scratch. Because I understood how systems communicate, learning DevOps concepts became more manageable.

# Assignment 4. Have you ever cut corners (unethical / dishonest / shortcut behavior — not necessarily illegal)? If yes, how did it make you feel?

### Important

You don't need to write the full story.

Focus on the feeling:

* guilt
* fear
* shame
* stress
* regret
* numbness
* etc.

This is about self-awareness, not judgment.

### Answer Format

**Yes / No**

If Yes:

**What emotion did you feel?** (minimum 50–100 words)

## Answer

Yes

There have been occasions where I chose the easier path instead of giving my best effort, particularly when I felt pressured by time or workload. Although the shortcut helped me finish the task more quickly, it did not leave me feeling satisfied. I experienced guilt because I knew the outcome did not fully represent my abilities or standards. More importantly, I realized that shortcuts often create additional work later when mistakes need to be corrected. That experience reinforced the importance of integrity, thoroughness, and accountability in both my personal and professional life. Today, I try to complete tasks properly the first time, even if it requires more effort.
